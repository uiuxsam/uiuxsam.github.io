Feasibility analysis and The decision

After the A/B Testing, I had a meeting with the developers to finalize the design based on the most feasible option within given time and budget. According to the developers, wireframe option 3 is the most viable option considering the 3rd party platform they are using. Option 2 is doable but it would take triple the time and effort which is beyond the current budget and timeline. Therefore, the decision was to stick with wireframe option 3. 


Final Prototype and Specifications

Interactions

2nd Screen: Current Issues

- The dropdown fields do not have type-ahead. The user has to scroll through the bottom to find desired state, institution or library name.

- Pain points are shown in the red rectangle.

Problems

2nd Screen- Current State: Selection options are available in dropdowns and one item can be selected at a time. Only one dropdown is visible when the user lands on this screen. Other dropdowns are visible upon selection from the first dropdown. This is a tedious process.

Challenges

Assigning Institution Level Administrators: Assigning administrators at colleges to manage the accounts and businesses in their parent institution using the current Library Links system, is causing errors in 50% of the cases. It has 4 steps to complete the process.

1st screen: Selecting the users from a table

2nd screen: Selection options. I will work on this screen to solve existing issues.

3rd screen: Review selections

4th screen: Confirmation modal

Solution ideas:

1. Make the selection task easy and reduce the number of dropdowns.

2. Use alternative options to make all the selection fields visible so that the user can choose easily.

1. High Cost of Error: Users are making mistakes by choosing wrong options from the dropdown. When they figure out the error they have to go back and fix it. It creates confusion among users who wrongfully get notified.

2. Time Requirement: Many selection options are available in the dropdowns and one item can be selected at a time for each of them. Also the dropdowns are dependent on each other.

Library Links is a web application that allows the administrators at colleges to manage their customer institutions as well as student accounts. The application is using a third party software to accommodate the requirements.

Guerrilla Testing: Because of time limitation and no budget for a comprehensive user research, I did a guerrilla study with two administrator level users and two customers. This helped me to identify the problem areas and pain points and come up with actionable ideas.
